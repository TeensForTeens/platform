ERA Councils: The Three Pillars of an Engaged Constituency

Education: The Council to Foster Political Awareness

The Education Council spearheads our efforts to promote political education. It directs activities identifying and disseminating relevant information regarding local, state, national elections, and political advocacy. The Education Council further advises on efficient practices for establishing and facilitating access to political literature pertinent to candidates, issues, and PAC information. The education activities of this council are paramount to building and maintaining an informed youth constituency.

Representation: The Council to Expand Representation in Government

The Representation Council bolsters the voice of Americans in government. The profound lack of political concern regarding the views of Americans has hindered their involvement in policy decisions. This council works to reverse that trend by seeking out opportunities to expand the American people’s voice in government and provide a forum for political expression. By promoting representation in government the Representation Council improves the legitimacy of American democracy and illustrates the importance of civic involvement.

Action: The Council to Implement Legislative Strategies

The Action Council pioneers action to advance legislation that will meaningfully remove obstacles to political involvement. This council focuses primarily fostering new avenues for a greater participation in government through legislative actions. The legislative implications of the Action Council have far reaching effects for representation thereby encouraging political participation.

Our Story

The Unified Democracy Political Action Committee was founded by Benjamin Burstein in February 2015 to encourage increased civic involvement in his home city of Miami Beach, Florida. Originally by the name {{ config.APP_NAME }}, the Unified Democracy PAC played a significant role in helping increase political participation among students in South Florida.

After a year of working with candidates in South Florida, the potential to spread the message of creating a better democracy was working. Voter turnout among young people was increasing, voter registration numbers were at all time highs, and the Unified Democracy PAC quickly became one of the largest political organizations in the country with 105 volunteers contributing over 8,000 hours of their time to candidates supported by the PAC.

The Unified Democracy PAC helped increase voter turnout in numerous local elections:

  • November 2015 city of Miami election
  • November 2015 city of Miami Beach election
  • November 2015 city of Hialeah election
  • January 2016 Florida City election
  • February 2016 city of South Miami election
  • March 2016 city of Miami Beach special election
  • March 2016 city of Miami referendum

In early 2016, the Unified Democracy PAC began its transition to become a national organization. We now have 11 state affiliates under the organization with more than one hundred students working for the PAC.
